Start With Nguyenlananh.com in 15 Minutes

This article is for people who want to start seriously without getting tangled up. You will know what Nguyenlananh.com is, where to begin, and how to keep moving with the system.

1) Who Nguyenlananh.com is for

You are in the right place if you are in any of these states:

  • You do a lot but feel you are not moving forward.
  • You know you need to change but do not know where to start.
  • You want a clearer rhythm in your life: less noise, less drain, less doing-for-the-sake-of-doing.
  • You need a small but real system of practice, not generic advice.

2) What you receive when you join

Nguyenlananh.com does not run like a blog. It runs like a journey system:

  • You read exactly what you need to read, with no scattered detours.
  • You practice one small thing each day, around ten to thirty minutes.
  • You check in to turn content into behavior.
  • You complete an exercise to unlock the next lesson. This is progressive unlock — it keeps you from being abandoned mid-way.
  • You see your own progress so you know where you are going.

You do not need to do a lot. You need to do it steadily and truthfully.

3) Where to begin (three options)

Option A: Free

If you want to try first:

  • Read the foundational pieces at /en/bai-viet/
  • See the overview at /en/hanh-trinh/ and /en/phuong-phap/
  • Pick one piece, do one small action, and stop there.

Option B: Seven-day plan (one US dollar per day)

This is the easiest entry for newcomers because the rhythm is short and clear:

  • A seven-day path
  • One lesson opens per day
  • One check-in per day
  • Progressive unlock

Option C: Monthly or yearly plan

If you want to go deeper and hold a longer rhythm:

  • A library of lessons
  • A longer practice path
  • Weekly and monthly check-ins
  • The yearly plan saves you the equivalent of two months compared to paying monthly

If you are unsure, choose the seven-day plan.

4) How to start, step by step

  1. Step 1: Open /en/join/
  2. Step 2: Choose a plan: seven-day, monthly, or yearly
  3. Step 3: Pay using the available rails: Stripe, PayPal, or QR-style bank transfer in Vietnam
  4. Step 4: Sign in via the magic link sent to your email, or sign in with Google
  5. Step 5: Go to /en/members/start/ and complete Day 1

If you can do only one thing today, do Day 1.

5) The daily practice rhythm (the core)

Each day takes only four steps:

  1. Observe (five minutes): What am I avoiding today.
  2. Small action (ten to twenty minutes): tidy a corner, finish one thing whole, or open five minutes for something hard.
  3. Write (three to five minutes): note what you saw.
  4. Check in: answer the question prompted by the system.

Do not try to make it pretty. Make it real.

6) Why progressive unlock works

You will not be flooded with content. The system opens with rhythm:

  • You do the exercise and check in: the next piece unlocks.
  • You skip a check-in: the system sends a gentle email reminder, and your progress slows.

The aim is not to control you. The aim is to keep the rhythm so you do not slip back into the old loop.

7) Opportunities for early members

In the early phase, early members usually have advantages:

  • You may be invited into small experiment groups.
  • You may be asked to give feedback that shapes the lesson system.
  • You may have the chance to become a content collaborator if it fits.
  • Long term, you may be invited into mentor cohorts or deeper groups once the system has enough operational evidence.

The only thing you need to do to deserve this opportunity is: keep the rhythm truthfully.

Closing

If you want to begin for real, choose one entry point:

  • Begin the seven-day plan at /en/join/
  • Or open Day 1 at /en/members/start/ after signing in

You can keep reading. Or begin your real journey here.
